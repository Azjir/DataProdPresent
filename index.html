The Problem

Obesity in the United States

  • Obesity is one of the biggest health concerns in the United states
  • According to the CDC(Center for Disease Control and Prevention) more than 1 in 3 americans is obese
  • Obesity is related to increased rate of heart disease, stroke and type 2 diabetes

Knowledge is Power

  • Body Mass Index(BMI) is easy to calculate and can give an estimate of whether someone is obese, healthy or underweight
  • An easy way to calculate BMI will give people information about themselves
  • Having this information, people may use it to figure out if they need to lose or even gain weight.
  • A healthy BMI is between 18.5 and 25. Below 18.5 is considered underweight and above 25 is considered overweight. Over 30 BMI is considered obese.

Introducing the BMI calculator

  • The BMI calculator is a simple tool that takes in height and mass and outputs BMI
  • BMI is calculated by dividing the weight by the square of the height
    • In the case of imperial measurements this number has to be multiplied by 703 to make the units work out
  • For example, a person who is 68 inches tall(5' 8") and weighs 160 lb, the BMI would be calculated in this way:
height <- 68
weight <- 160
BMI <- (weight*703)/(height)^2
  • Therefore their BMI would be: 24.3252595, right on the verge of overweight.
